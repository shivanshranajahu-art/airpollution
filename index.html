🔬 What is this project?

This project analyzes the spatial and temporal distribution of Nitrogen Dioxide (NO₂) over India using satellite data from the Sentinel-5P satellite. All processing is done on Google Earth Engine, a free cloud platform by Google that stores petabytes of satellite imagery. The project compares NO₂ levels for 2023 and 2024 to find pollution trends.

⚠️ Why is NO₂ important?

Nitrogen Dioxide is produced by vehicle exhaust, power plants, and industrial activities. It causes respiratory diseases, cardiovascular problems, and contributes to smog and acid rain. Monitoring NO₂ from space helps scientists track pollution hotspots and evaluate the impact of government environmental policies across large regions efficiently.

Key Findings
Important observations from the satellite data analysis
🔴

High Pollution Hotspots

Indo-Gangetic Plain covering Uttar Pradesh, Bihar, and Delhi shows highest NO₂ due to dense traffic and industrial activity.

❄️

Winter Peak Pollution

November to February shows highest NO₂ levels due to temperature inversions that trap pollutants near the ground surface.

🌧️

Monsoon Cleans the Air

June to August shows lowest NO₂ because rainfall washes pollutants from the atmosphere naturally.

🏭

Industrial Zones Visible

Jharkhand, West Bengal, and coastal Maharashtra show clear industrial pollution hotspots on the satellite map.

📈

Year-on-Year Trend

Comparison between 2023 and 2024 reveals annual pollution trends and impact of environmental policy changes.

🌿

Clean Southern India

Southern states and northeastern India show significantly lower NO₂ due to less industry and better green cover.

Q1. What is Google Earth Engine? ▼
Google Earth Engine is a cloud-based platform by Google that provides free access to petabytes of satellite imagery and geospatial datasets. It allows scientists to perform large-scale environmental analysis without needing powerful local hardware.
Q2. What is Sentinel-5P satellite? ▼
Sentinel-5P is a satellite launched by ESA in 2017 as part of the Copernicus programme. It carries the TROPOMI instrument which measures atmospheric gases including NO₂, SO₂, CO, and ozone with spatial resolution of 3.5 x 5.5 km.
Q3. What is NO₂ and why is it dangerous? ▼
Nitrogen Dioxide (NO₂) is produced by vehicle exhaust, power plants, and industries. It causes respiratory diseases, contributes to smog and acid rain, and is a major indicator of air pollution levels in urban and industrial areas.
Q4. What dataset did you use? ▼
We used COPERNICUS/S5P/OFFL/L3_NO2 from Google Earth Engine. OFFL means offline processed high-quality data. It measures tropospheric NO₂ column number density in moles per square meter (mol/m²).
Q5. Why are NO₂ levels higher in winter? ▼
In winter, cold temperatures cause temperature inversions where warm air traps cold air near the ground. This prevents pollutants from dispersing upward, causing NO₂ to accumulate near the surface in higher concentrations.
Q6. What is reduceRegion in your code? ▼
reduceRegion is a GEE function that applies a mathematical operation like mean or median over all pixels within a geographic region. We used it to calculate the average NO₂ value across entire India for each month.
Q7. Which areas show highest NO₂ in India? ▼
The highest NO₂ is in the Indo-Gangetic Plain covering Uttar Pradesh, Bihar, and Delhi. Industrial areas of Jharkhand and West Bengal also show high pollution due to heavy industrial and thermal power plant activity.
Q8. What is the significance of time-based comparison? ▼
Time-based comparison helps identify whether pollution is increasing or decreasing year by year. It helps evaluate the impact of government environmental policies and provides data for future pollution forecasting and urban planning.
Q9. Why did you use Python instead of JavaScript? ▼
Python provides better libraries for data analysis, graph generation, and report creation. Libraries like Matplotlib, Folium, and ReportLab make it easier to create professional visualizations and reports compared to JavaScript-based GEE editor.
Q10. What is TROPOMI? ▼
TROPOMI stands for TROPOspheric Monitoring Instrument. It is the main sensor onboard the Sentinel-5P satellite. It measures multiple atmospheric pollutants including NO₂, SO₂, CO, CH₄, and ozone at very high spatial resolution.
